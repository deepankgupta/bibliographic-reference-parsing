<p>Ann. Natal Mus.     Vol. 16&nbsp;Pages 1—6&nbsp;Pietermaritzburg     June, 1964</p>
<p>Redescription of the type species of the spider genera</p>
<p>Artonis, Chorizopes and Mecynidis (Araneae : Araneidae and</p>
<p>Linyphiidae)</p>
<p>by</p>
<p>Herbert W. Levi</p>
<p>(Museum of Comparative Zoology, Harvard University)</p>
<p>Redescription of the numerous old spider species is often far more important<br>than the description and naming of additional new ones. This is particularly true<br>of the types of generic names. The species redescribed here had been placed in<br>the Theridiidae by many authors. A recent revision of the genera of the family<br>Theridiidae (Levi and Levi, 1962) indicated the erroneous placement of several of<br>these genera, but redescription of the type species was not within the scope of that<br>paper. These type species of the araneids and linyphiids are redescribed here,<br>with the exception of Paurotylus depressus Tullgren, 1910, of which I examined some<br>male specimens at the Musée Royal de l'Afrique Centrale in Tervuren last August,<br>with Dr. P. L. G. Benoit, and we found that it belongs to the Gasteracanthinae<br>(Araneidae). Unfortunately, it is difficult to match males and females in this group.<br>Dr. Benoit has redescribed the male type in his revision of African Gasteracanthinae.</p>
<p>Finding some of the specimens studied here in the large spider collections is a<br>difficult, time-consuming task. The labels, particularly of some of the older col-<br>lections, are barely legible although the spiders are in excellent condition. For the<br>laborious job of finding the specimens and sending them to me I would like to thank<br>Prof. G. C. Varley and Mr. E. Taylor of the Hope Department of Zoology, Oxford,<br>Prof. M. Vachon and Mr. J. F. Jézéquel of the Muséum National d'Histoire Naturelle,<br>Paris, and Prof. E. Tortonese and the staff of the entomology department of the<br>Museo Civico di Storia Naturale in Genova. A National Science Foundation<br>grant (G-4317) made possible the initial examination of the specimens; a National<br>Institutes of Health grant (E-1944) aided in completion of this short paper.</p>

<p>Mecynidis dentipalpis Simon</p>
<p>Mecynidis dentipalpis Simon, 1894, Histoire Naturelle des Araignées, 1: 507, figs. 511, 512, S. Male<br>lectotype here designated from Transvaal in the Muséum National d'Histoire Naturelle,<br>Paris, examined.</p>
<p>Description. — Carapace, sternum dark brown ; abdomen gray with sclerotized<br>areas brown, a lighter spot on each side of dorsal scutum. Shape of carapace as in<br>Figures 1, 2. Eyes subequal in size. Anterior median eyes one and one-half dia-<br>meters apart, two and one-half diameters from laterals. Posterior median eyes one<br>and one-half diameters apart, two and one-half from laterals. Anterior and posterior<br>lateral eyes touching. Height of clypeus equals about two and one-half diameters<br>of anterior median eyes. Chelicerae with four teeth on anterior margin, two pos-<br>terior (fig. 3). Sternum triangular, pointed behind. Abdomen longer than wide<br>or high (fig. 2); anterior two-thirds of abdomen dorsum covered by a weakly<br>sclerotized scutum.   Legs without spines;   thorns on femora of palpus (fig. 5).</p>
 <p>Figs. 1—6. Mecynidis dentipalpis Simon, male. 1. Carapace. 2. Male, lateral view with palpus and<br>first leg on right side and second leg on left. 3. Left chelicera, posterior view. 4—6. Left palpus.<br>4. Mesal view. 5. Ventral view. 6 Ectal view.</p>

<p>Artonis bituberculata (Thorell)</p>
<p>Anania bituberculata Thorell, 1895, Descriptive Catalog of the Spiders of Burma, p. 148. Female<br>holotype from Tharrawaddy, Burma in the Museo Civico di Storia Naturale, Genova,<br>examined.</p>
<p>Note. — The specimen has a label indicating &quot;Palon. Fea&quot;. The description<br>indicates that it was collected in Tharrawaddy by Oates.</p>
<p>Description. — Carapace, sternum brown. Coxae yellow. Legs light brown,<br>indications of darker bands on distal segments.   Abdomen with little pigment:</p>

<p>a gray ring around spinnerets; some black pigment on tubercles and in two trans-<br>verse lines behind tubercles; some indistinct silvery pigment spots in two rows, one<br>on each side of dorsum, and in two longitudinal rows on venter. Carapace heavily<br>sclerotized. Posterior median eyes slightly larger than anterior medians, lateral<br>eyes slightly smaller than anterior medians. Anterior medians one and one-half<br>diameters apart. Posterior medians one diameter apart and a little more than one<br>diameter from anteriors. Laterals touching each other. Chelicerae with four teeth<br>on anterior margin (fig. 9); on posterior, a carina at base of fang, two teeth and one<br>denticle. Abdomen longer than wide or high, with two humps (figs. 7, 8) and many<br>small, sclerotized spots, the bases of short setae, and a few slightly larger sclerotized<br>spots. Palpus of the female with a claw. Total length 3.9 mm. Carapace 1.4<br>mm long, 1.0 mm wide. First patella and tibia, 1.3 mm; second, 1.2 mm; third,<br>0.8 mm. Fourth femur, 1.4 mm; patella and tibia, 1.5 mm; metatarsus, 1.0 mm;<br>tarsus, 0.5 mm.</p>
<p>The epigynum (fig. 11) is a flat plate with the opening on each side framed in a<br>heavier sclerotized area. A median scape is almost transparent. There are two<br>pairs of sclerotized seminal receptacles (fig. 10).</p>

<p>Chorizopes frontalis O. P.- Cambridge</p>
<p>Chorizoopes frontalis O. P. — Cambridge, 1870, Proc. zool. Soc. Lond., p. 738, pl. 44, fig. 6, 9. Two<br>female syntypes from Ceylon in the Hope Department of Entomology, University<br>Museum, Oxford, examined.</p>
<p>Description — Carapace dark brown, hairy, chelicerae dark brown, sternum light<br>brown, legs yellowish brown, with darker grey rings. Abdomen light brown with<br>grey maculations and two white dorsal spots (fig. 13) or sometimes a single large<br>white dorsal patch; black patch on each side above spinnerets. Carapace with<br>a hump in thoracic region (fig. 14). Anterior median eyes slightly larger than pos-<br>teriors, laterals slightly smaller than posterior medians. Anterior median eyes a<br>little more than a diameter apart; posterior median eyes one and one-half diameters<br>apart. Anterior median eyes one diameter from posterior medians. Lateral eyes<br>touching. Chelicerae with a cluster of teeth on anterior margin (fig. 12); on pos-<br>terior margin a loose fleshy extension attached to base of fang (fig. 15). Abdomen<br>with three dorsal posterior humps. Epigynum a slightly convex plate, partly trans-<br>parent ; the posterior portion, containing a pair of black spots, is slightly concave</p>

<p>Note. — The male described came from a different collection than did the syn-<br>types, but was collected with females. The carapace of the male is slightly wider<br>behind than that of the female figured, and the abdominal humps are more pro-<br>nounced.</p>
<p>Males and females marked Chorizopes frontalis by Simon, probably coming<br>from Ceylon and preserved in the Museum National d'Histoire Naturelle in Paris,<br>are a very similar but probably distinct species. The thoracic hump is less pro-<br>nounced, the epigynum is similar but less transparent, and the shape of the palpal<br>sclerites differ.  The chelicerae are as in C. frontalis.</p>
